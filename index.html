      FAQ

      How long has Dranima been in development, and what is already done?

      Dranima has been in active development for a little over five years now (development started around 2020–2021). Most of the core documentation and planning is already finalized, and the main focus at the moment is the battle system, which is one of the most important gameplay features.

      How many years do you think it will take to finish the game?

      It's difficult to give a precise timeline. An Alpha or Beta version may appear in the coming years, but there is currently no fixed release date for the final version. Development is progressing step by step, and updates will be shared whenever there is any meaningful progress to show.

      Is it an indie solo game?

      Both yes and no. I started the project alone and have been working on it ever since. Currently, the project is self-funded and developed with help from several collaborators, mainly concept artists. The current priority is securing funding — you can also read about those who contributed (check "Credits" at the top of the page to see who participated in the development of this project).

      How can people support the project right now?

      The best way to support the project right now is by following its development and sharing it with others who might be interested. Since Dranima is still in development, visibility and community feedback help a lot. In the future, there will also be opportunities to support the project more directly when the game reaches a more advanced stage.

      Will there be multiplayer?

      Probably not. Dranima is primarily designed as a story-driven JRPG focused on a single-player experience. While we have considered a few ideas for multiplayer features or community-oriented things, they are unlikely to be implemented even in future updates.

      How often will there be updates?

      There will be regular updates. Most of them will focus on bug fixes, balancing improvements, minor quality-of-life changes, and small pieces of content. Larger updates may also happen every few months.

      — Alex Bourbon, creator of Dranima
